ü§ñ AI & Predictions

How does AI prediction work?

The platform uses two complementary prediction methods:

1. Indicator-Based Prediction (Rule-Based)

  • Analyzes RSI (Relative Strength Index)
  • Checks MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
  • Evaluates EMA alignment (9, 20, 50, 100, 200)
  • Assesses Bollinger Bands position
  • Considers volume trends
  • Combines scores into BUY/SELL/HOLD signal

2. AI Model Prediction (Machine Learning)

  • Uses scikit-learn RandomForest algorithms
  • Trained on historical OHLCV data and computed indicators
  • Predicts direction (up/down) and magnitude (price change %)
  • Provides confidence scores

Note: Both methods are educational demonstrations. Real-world trading requires more sophisticated models, risk management, and continuous monitoring.

‚ö†Ô∏è Prediction accuracy varies significantly and should NOT be relied upon for real trading.

Factors affecting accuracy:

  • Market conditions (trending vs. ranging)
  • Data quality and freshness
  • Model training data size
  • Sudden news events or black swan events
  • Low liquidity or manipulation

The AI models are trained on limited historical data and use simplified features. Professional trading systems use far more sophisticated approaches, including sentiment analysis, order flow, macro indicators, and ensemble models.

What are EMA signals and Golden/Death crosses?

EMA (Exponential Moving Average) is a trend-following indicator that gives more weight to recent prices.

üìà Golden Cross (Bullish)

Occurs when a faster EMA (e.g., EMA 50) crosses above a slower EMA (e.g., EMA 200). This is traditionally interpreted as a bullish signal, suggesting an uptrend may be beginning.

üìâ Death Cross (Bearish)

Occurs when a faster EMA crosses below a slower EMA. This is traditionally interpreted as a bearish signal, suggesting a downtrend may be starting.

Important: These are lagging indicators and not always reliable. They work best in trending markets but can produce false signals in ranging markets.

ü§ñ Trading Bots

What is a Grid Trading Bot?

A Grid Bot creates multiple buy and sell orders at predefined price levels within a specified range. It profits from price volatility by repeatedly buying low and selling high as the price oscillates.

When to Use:

  • ‚úÖ Sideways/ranging markets with clear support and resistance
  • ‚úÖ Low-volatility assets moving within a predictable range
  • ‚úÖ When you expect price to stay within bounds

When NOT to Use:

  • ‚ùå Strong trending markets (up or down)
  • ‚ùå High-volatility assets that can break out of range
  • ‚ùå Low-liquidity pairs with large spreads

Tip: The platform's EMA signals can help identify when the market is sideways (best for Grid) vs. trending (best for DCA).

What is a DCA (Dollar-Cost Averaging) Bot?

DCA is an investment strategy where you buy (or sell) a fixed amount at regular intervals or when price drops by a certain percentage. This smooths out market volatility and reduces the risk of buying at the peak.

When to Use DCA BUY Bots:

  • ‚úÖ During downtrends to accumulate at lower prices
  • ‚úÖ When you believe the asset will recover long-term
  • ‚úÖ To build a position gradually without timing the market

When to Use DCA SELL Bots:

  • ‚úÖ During uptrends to take profits gradually
  • ‚úÖ To exit a position without selling everything at once

Risk Warning:

DCA does NOT guarantee profit. In a continuously falling market (e.g., a project going to zero), you will accumulate losses. Always use stop-loss limits and only invest in fundamentally sound assets.

How do trend-aware bots work?

The platform integrates EMA signals into bot decision-making:

Grid Bots:

  • Paused during strong trends (BUY/SELL signals or Golden/Death crosses)
  • Only run during sideways/neutral markets
  • Prevents losses from breakouts above/below grid range

DCA Bots:

  • BUY bots only execute during uptrends or Golden Cross
  • SELL bots only execute during downtrends or Death Cross
  • Skipped if trend doesn't match bot direction

This makes bots "smarter" by aligning them with market conditions.

Can I lose money with automated bots?

‚ö†Ô∏è YES. Bots do not guarantee profit and can lose money.

Common ways bots lose money:

  • Grid bots in trending markets (price breaks out of range)
  • DCA bots in continuously falling markets (buying more of a losing asset)
  • High exchange fees eating into small profits
  • Slippage in low-liquidity markets
  • Incorrect parameter configuration

Risk Management Tips:

  • Always set stop-loss limits
  • Start with small amounts and testnet first
  • Monitor bots regularly - they're not "set and forget"
  • Use appropriate bot type for market conditions
  • Don't over-leverage or use funds you can't afford to lose

How do I connect my exchange account?
  1. Navigate to the Exchange Accounts page
  2. Choose your exchange (Binance, Bybit, etc.)
  3. Create API keys on your exchange:
    • Log into your exchange account
    • Go to API Management
    • Create new API key with appropriate permissions
    • Enable IP restrictions for security
  4. Enter API Key and Secret in the form
  5. Check "Use Testnet" for safe testing
  6. Click "Link Exchange Account"
  7. Click "Test Connection" to verify it works

Security Warning: Never share your API secret with anyone. Enable IP restrictions and use read-only permissions if you're only fetching data.

Why are my predictions showing "Not enough data"?

This error occurs when there isn't sufficient historical price data in the database to calculate indicators like EMA 200 (which requires at least 200 candles).

How to Fix:

  1. Click the "üîÑ Sync Price History Now" button in the error banner
  2. Wait for the system to fetch historical data from the exchange
  3. Refresh the page

The system automatically syncs price data every 2 hours, but you can trigger it manually anytime.
